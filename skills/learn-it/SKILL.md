# Learn-It — Feynman Technique

Teach any concept so clearly that a smart 12-year-old could explain it back.
The Feynman Technique in 4 phases: Simple explanation → Expose gaps → Rebuild simply → Cement with examples.

---

## Phase 1 — Plain Language Explanation
Explain the concept as if talking to someone who has never heard of it.
- No jargon without immediate definition
- One core idea per sentence
- Lead with *what it is*, then *why it exists*, then *how it works*
- Target length: 3–5 short paragraphs

---

## Phase 2 — Find the Hard Parts
Identify the 2–3 points where most people get confused or make wrong assumptions.
Label them clearly:

> **Common sticking point**: [misconception or tricky part]
> **What's actually happening**: [simple reframe]

---

## Phase 3 — Rebuild with an Analogy
Pick one powerful real-world analogy that maps onto the concept's structure.
- The analogy must match the *mechanism*, not just the surface
- Show where the analogy holds and where it breaks down
- If the concept is highly technical, use a second simpler analogy

---

## Phase 4 — Cement with Examples
Give 2–3 concrete examples that progress in complexity:
1. **Trivial example** — the simplest possible case, zero ambiguity
2. **Real-world example** — something the user encounters in life or work
3. **Edge case** — a surprising or non-obvious instance that tests real understanding

End with one check-for-understanding question:
> *"To check: can you tell me [simple test question about the concept]?"*

---

## Rules
- Never use the concept's name to define itself ("recursion is when something recurses")
- If the topic has sub-concepts, teach the most foundational one first
- Adapt depth to the user's apparent background — a developer asking about TCP gets different examples than a student asking about TCP
- If the concept is broad (e.g. "machine learning"), scope it: *"There's a lot here — I'll focus on [specific angle]. Want a different angle after?"*
- Keep the full response readable in one sitting — aim for 400–700 words
